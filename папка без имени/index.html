How heat waves are formed?

Heat waves result from the interaction of factors of different nature. In recent years, global warming has been a key factor making heat waves more frequent and intense in most regions of the world.

Threats Posed by Extreme Heat

Heat waves have a significant impact on the environment. Here is how it happens:
  • Evaporation of water bodies: High temperatures cause water bodies to evaporate rapidly, which can cause drought and impair access to water resources.
  • Damage to ecosystems: Ecosystems become vulnerable. High temperatures can affect wildlife, plants and animals.
  • Wildfire risk: Heat waves increase the likelihood of wildfires due to dryness and high temperatures

    • Human health

    Heat waves can have serious effects on human health. Here are some of them:
  • Heat Stroke: This is a condition where the body temperature rises to dangerous levels due to prolonged exposure to high temperatures. Symptoms include headache, heavy sweating, weakness and even loss of consciousness.
  • Dehydration: During a heat wave, the body rapidly loses moisture through sweat. This can lead to dehydration, which is dangerous to your health.
  • Respiratory problems: High temperatures can degrade air quality, which can affect breathing.

    • Resilience for extreme heat

    Adapting to heat waves is essential to protect human health and reduce negative effects. Here are some tips:
  • Stay cool:
    1. Avoid prolonged exposure to the sun.
    2. Use an air conditioner or fan.
  • Drink enough water: Dehydration can be dangerous. Drink water regularly.
  • Cool off:
    1. Use wet towels on your body.
    2. Take cool showers or baths.

    Enviroment

    Adaptation to climate change is an important step towards a sustainable future for cities. Here are a few ways cities are paying attention to adapting to hot conditions:
  • Increasing green spaces: Planting more trees and creating green spaces on walls and roofs helps cool cities. Green spaces absorb heat and create a pleasant microclimate
  • Blue infrastructure: Creating water features such as fountains, ponds and rivers helps to cool cities. Water absorbs heat and creates a pleasant microclimate
  • Use of cool materials: Introducing materials that reflect sunlight and do not heat up as much can help reduce temperatures in cities. This includes green roofs, reflective roofs and cool road surfaces

    • There are a few examples of cities that are innovating to fight the heat:

  • The first one is Singapore. Singapure is making extensive use of green roofs, vertical gardens and rooftop parks. This helps to cool the city environment and improve air quality.
  • The second one is Melbourne .Melbourne has introduced a 'Green Roof Strategy', encouraging building owners to install green roof coverings. This reduces the heat load and mitigates the climate.
  • The third one is Porto. The city has created the "Green Corridor", a network of green spaces, parks and gardens to help reduce temperatures and improve the comfort of citizens.
